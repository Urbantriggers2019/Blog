Power Grid

-Transmission network: consists mainly of power lines with 300 or 420 kV voltage, but in some parts of the country also power lines with 132 kV voltage are included.

-Regional network: is the regional network, which is the link between the transmission network and the network level below. Normal voltage levels in the distribution grid are 132 and 66 kV.

-The distribution network: provides end-users (households, services, and industry). The distribution grid includes voltages ranging from 22 kV and all the way down to the 230 V low voltage grid, which supplies ordinary households.
